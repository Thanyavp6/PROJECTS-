Footstep Power Generation Using Piezoelectric Sensors
📌 Overview

This project focuses on generating electrical energy from human footsteps using piezoelectric sensors. It presents an innovative and sustainable solution to harvest wasted mechanical energy in high-footfall areas like malls, railway stations, and sidewalks.
The system converts the pressure exerted by walking into electrical energy, which is then processed, stored, and used to power low-energy devices such as LEDs and IoT systems.

🌍 Motivation
With increasing energy demand and depletion of conventional resources, there is a strong need for renewable and eco-friendly energy solutions. A large amount of energy is wasted daily through human movement.

This project aims to:
Utilize human kinetic energy
Promote green energy solutions
Support smart city infrastructure

⚙️ Working Principle
The system works based on the piezoelectric effect, where mechanical stress generates electrical voltage.

Step-by-step process:

Footstep Pressure
When a person steps on the platform, pressure is applied to piezoelectric sensors.
Energy Generation
Sensors convert mechanical energy into AC voltage.
Rectification
A bridge rectifier converts AC to DC.
Filtering
Capacitors smooth the fluctuating voltage.
Voltage Regulation
A voltage regulator ensures stable output.
Energy Storage
Energy is stored in a rechargeable battery or capacitor.
Utilization
Stored energy powers devices like LEDs, sensors, etc.
